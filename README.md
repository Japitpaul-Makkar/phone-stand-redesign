# phone-stand-redesign

Commercially available phone stands commonly suffer from three fundamental design flaws: cradle geometries that obstruct the speaker grilles located at the bottom of modern smartphones, undersized cradle tabs that fail to securely grip and support the phone, and base designs that extend only front-to-back, leaving the stand vulnerable to side-to-side tipping. These shortcomings compromise both the functional and ergonomic value of the product. This project aims to redesign a consumer phone stand from the ground up using SolidWorks CAD software, addressing all three failure modes through deliberate mechanical design decisions. The redesigned stand will feature a fork-style cradle geometry that supports the phone at its lower corners while leaving the bottom speaker area fully unobstructed, enlarged and reinforced support tabs for a more secure grip, and a base that extends both front-to-back and side-to-side to maximize surface contact and tip resistance in all directions. The redesigned stand will retain full mechanical adjustability — including tilt angle, height, and base extension — matching the functional range of the reference product. The final design will be validated through a complete professional drawing package, a bill of materials, and a 3D-printed physical prototype tested against defined performance requirements.
